Creating is an essential part of my lifestyle. It is the best way I know to process my thoughts, and the world around. I paint to understand, and ultimately to let go of the emotions I am living through.

As human beings, we often have similar experiences in life. I show my work, so that other people can resonate with the work too, and maybe have their closure through my art.

I like to have a sense of immediacy and honesty in my work, which means that if I stretch painting one piece for too long, I lose my momentum. I tend to finish a piece in several days with long, intense hours, rather than contemplating between the sessions and adjusting the painting bit by bit, but some pieces may take months or years to complete.

Themes that appear in my artwork often are lucid dreaming, relationship with reality, beauty of daily moments, and emotional and psychological experiences of people. These are the things that I often reflect on in my daily life, and undoubtedly, they feed into my work.
